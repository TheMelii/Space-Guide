Our Solar System

In the center of our solar system is the sun, it's the biggest star in our solar system. Our solar system has eight planets and those planets orbit the sun.

The first four planets, as well as the dwarf planet Ceres and some other astronomical objects (e.g. Asteroids) build the inner Solar System, followed by the Asteroid Belt who seperates those planets from the outer solar system. The last four planets, as well as other astronomical objects, build then the outer Solar System. They are followed by the Kuiper Belt in which the other known dwarf planets, including Pluto are located.

In general you can say the more distant a planet is away from sun, the longer it takes for it to orbit the sun once.

By now there have been discovered only five dwarf planets in our solar system. But assumebly there are still up to 50 other dwarf planats undiscovered in our solar system.

The major differenc between a planet and a dwarf planet is, that a dwarf planet has'nt cleared the neigborhood around its orbit, while a planet has.

Our Solar System orbits the galactical center of our galaxy-the milky way. It takes our SOlar System about 230m years to orbit the center of the Milky Way once.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet within our solar system and also the closest to the sun. Mercury has no moons.

By now no life has been found on Mercury and it's unlikely that life can exist on Mercury due to it's hot temperatures from up to 430°C and it's cold temperatures from down to -180°C.

Earth ♁

The earth is the planet that we all live on. It's the third planet from the sun and the only planet that living beings exist.

Within the inner Solar System and among the terrestrial planets, earth is the biggest planet.

So far it is the only planet that liquid water on the surface existsa and nearly 71% of the earth's surface is covered from water.

The earth's atmosphere contains mostly nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%). This atmosphere protects us also from meteroids, as the atmosphere breaks up most of the incoming meteroids.

Jupiter ♃

Jupiter is the biggest planet in our solar system, the fifth planet from the sun and the first planet in the outer solar system.

Jupiters atmosphere consists of hydrogen and helium, and is a gas giant. The swirls and stripes that can be seen on Jupiters surface are cloud layers above Jupiters surface.

The giant red spot on the surface is a giant storm that raged on for hundred of years.

Jupiter has a ring system and over 75 moons. Jupiter itself does'nt support life as we know it but some of its moons may hold oceans and water underneath their crust that could support it.
