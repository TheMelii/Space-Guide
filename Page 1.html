Our Solar System

In the center of our solar system is the sun, it's the biggest star in our solar system. Our solar system has eight planets and those planets orbit the sun.

The first four planets, as well as the dwarf planet Ceres and some other astronomical objects (e.g. Asteroids) build the inner Solar System, followed by the Asteroid Belt who seperates those planets from the outer solar system. The last four planets, as well as other astronomical objects, build then the outer Solar System. They are followed by the Kuiper Belt in which the other known dwarf planets, including Pluto are located.

In general you can say the more distant a planet is away from sun, the longer it takes for it to orbit the sun once.

By now there have been discovered only five dwarf planets in our solar system. But assumebly there are still up to 50 other dwarf planats undiscovered in our solar system.

The major differenc between a planet and a dwarf planet is, that a dwarf planet has'nt cleared the neigborhood around its orbit, while a planet has.

Our Solar System orbits the galactical center of our galaxy-the milky way. It takes our Solar System about 230m years to orbit the center of the Milky Way once.

Planets

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet within our solar system and also the closest to the sun. Mercury has no moons.

By now no life has been found on Mercury and it's unlikely that life can exist on Mercury due to it's hot temperatures from up to 430°C and it's cold temperatures from down to -180°C.

Venus

Venus is the brightest planet in our solar system. The surface of Venus is fully covered in a cloud layer.

The temperatures rises up to 500°C and the pressure is 90 times that of earth's air pressure.

Water does'nt exist on this planet.

Earth ♁

The earth is the planet that we all live on. It's the third planet from the sun and the only planet that living beings exist.

Within the inner Solar System and among the terrestrial planets, earth is the biggest planet.

So far it is the only planet that liquid water on the surface exists and nearly 71% of the earth's surface is covered by water.

The earth's atmosphere contains mostly nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%). This atmosphere protects us from meteroids, as the atmosphere breaks up most of the incoming meteroids. But an upper layer of ozone in the atmosphere protects as also from the sun, as it absorbs UV radiations and prevents most of it to reach the earth.

Jupiter ♃

Jupiter is the biggest planet in our solar system, the fifth planet from the sun and the first planet in the outer solar system.

Jupiters atmosphere consists of hydrogen and helium, and is a gas giant. The swirls and stripes that can be seen on Jupiters surface are cloud layers above Jupiters surface.

The giant red spot on the surface is a giant storm that raged on for hundred of years.

Jupiter has a ring system and over 75 moons. Jupiter itself does'nt support life as we know it but some of its moons may hold oceans and water underneath their crust that could support it.

Uranus ♅

Uranus is a giant ice planet. It's the seventh planet from the earth and was the first planet which was ever discovered.

Uranus is very unique due to the planet rotating on its side, rotating from east to west.

This planets 27 moons are named after the works of William Shakespeare and Alexander Pope.

The Voyager 2 was the only spacecraft that ever flew to uranus and discovered it from a distance, but no spacecraft ever orbited Uranus.

Pluto ♆

Pluto was first discovered in 1930 and considered as the ninth planet of our solar system.

It wasn't till 2006 when pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet, as Ceres, Eris and Makemake where classified as well as dwarf planets due to their simmilar size as Pluto and their smaller sizes as the other eight planets. This action caused great controversy.

Pluto's orbit is really unusual as it is eliptical and tilted. This fact causes a huge difference in the distances from Pluto to the Sun. Pluto at perihelion (it's nearest point to the sun) is 4.44b km and Pluto at aphelion (farthest point away from the sun) is 7.38b km.

Because of dwarf planet Pluto’s elliptical orbit, Pluto is sometimes closer to the Sun (and us) than Neptune is.

Pluto has five moons in total, but the most interesting moon is called Charon and is even big enough that Pluto and Charon orbit each other.

Haumea

Haumea is not shaped like a sphere, but more shaped like an egg. Due to this shape it was a long process till Heumea was declared as a dwarf planet.

Haumea has the fastest rotation of all equilibrium bodies in the whole solar system.

It's high likely that Haumea lost about 20% of its mass due to a collision. This giant collision also produced Hi'iaka and Namaka the two moons of Haumea.

This dwarf planet was discovered in 2005, but not considered a dwarf planet till 2008.
